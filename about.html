My name is Jean-Yves Papineau. I live in Embrun ON. I grew up in northern Ontario; I was born in Kapuskasing, and moved to Cochrane when I was in grade 4.


Once I graduated high school, I joined the military. After a fair bit of training, I joined the ranks of the Combat Engineers. This would take me to Edmonton AB where I would spend the majority of my 21-year military career.


As a Combat Engineer, I deployed to Afghanistan twice; once in 2002, and again in 2006.


About halfway through my career (in 2008), I realized I wanted something different. So I changed trades within the military; I became a Geomatics Technician (a cartographer).


This new trade required 2 years of training at Algonquin College. The remainder of my career was spent doing various domestic operations as a Geomatics Technician.


My military career got cut short due to several injuries. I am currently transitioning from military life to that of a civilian. I took an interest in photography and decided to attempt the Interactive Media Design program at Algonquin College.
